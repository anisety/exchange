title: "Market lifecycle"
description: "How market status affects what you can trade—and how to stay in sync when organizers change a market."
---

**Market `status`** is part of your **trading logic**, not just labels on a screen. Combine periodic [Get Markets](/rest/markets) calls with live **`market_state`** WebSocket messages so you react as soon as something changes.

**`admin_message`** is for human-readable notices; **`market_state`** is the **authoritative** metadata update for the market definition itself.

## Status values

Each row from [Get Markets](/rest/markets) includes **`status`**.

### `enabled`

- Market is **open** for trading.
- New orders are accepted; resting orders can match.
- The symbol participates in portfolio and settlement logic.

### `disabled`

- Market is **visible** but **new orders are rejected**.
- You can still query state.
- **Pause** new submissions until the market is **`enabled`** again.

### `settled`

- **No more trading** in that symbol.
- Organizers set a final **`settlement_price`**.
- Open **positions** flatten against that price; resting **orders** for that market are canceled.
- **PnL** for that market becomes **realized**.
- **Refresh** portfolio, positions, and fills from REST.

## Competition-wide trading halt

Organizers can turn off **submissions** for the **whole** exchange. You may still see live quotes while **new** orders are rejected—**always** honor **`reject`** messages from order entry, not only the book.

## Fields to read before you send orders

From [Get Markets](/rest/markets), watch at least:

- `market_id`
- `status`
- `tick_size`
- `min_order_quantity`
- `min_price`
- `max_price`
- `reference_price`
- `settlement_price` (once known)

**`tick_size`**, optional **`min_price`** / **`max_price`**, and **`min_order_quantity`** decide whether the server accepts your **`price`** and **`quantity`**.

## Suggested client behavior

1. Load markets at **startup**.
2. **Do not** place orders when **`status`** is **`disabled`** or **`settled`**.
3. Apply **`market_state`** immediately; after reconnect or **`resync_required`**, call [Get Markets](/rest/markets) again.
4. After **settlement**, re-bootstrap **account** state—balances and PnL can change in one tick.

## Rankings and finals

Organizer rankings are private. Use settlement as the point where **that market’s** PnL is **stable** on the server.
