**REST** is the right tool when you need a clear, request–response answer: after a disconnect, when you are unsure of local state, or when a market changes status. Use it alongside WebSocket; many teams use WebSocket for speed and REST for truth.

## Suggested first calls

1. [Get User](/rest/user) — confirm identity.
2. [Get Markets](/rest/markets) — symbols, status, tick size, minimum size.
3. [Get Portfolio](/rest/portfolio) — positions and how risk limits apply to you.
4. After reconnects or doubt, [Get Open Orders](/rest/open-orders) and [Get Fills](/rest/fills).
5. [Create Order](/rest/submit-order), [Amend Order](/rest/amend-order), [Cancel Order](/rest/cancel-order) when you want HTTP order flow.

## Request conventions

- **`side`:** `BUY` or `SELL`
- **`order_type`:** `limit` or `market` (default `limit`)
- **`price`**, **`quantity`**, **`remaining`:** unsigned integers
- **Market orders:** send **`price: 0`**; the engine matches against the opposite side of the book
- **Position style:** you may buy or sell from flat, go long or short, subject to risk limits
- **Risk:** default worst-case net exposure per market is **±1000** contracts (unless organizers assign a different role)
- **Rate limit:** authenticated REST + authenticated WebSocket trading share **500 ops / 10s** by default; see [Rate limits](/reference/rate-limits)
- **Filters:** [Get Open Orders](/rest/open-orders) and [Get Fills](/rest/fills) accept optional **`market`** query parameter
- **Recovery:** when in doubt, prefer REST to realign your client with the server
